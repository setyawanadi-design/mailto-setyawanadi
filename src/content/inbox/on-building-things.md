There's something deeply satisfying about watching a system you built actually work in the real world.

I've spent years working in **field operations** — coordinating deployments, managing teams across multiple sites, and making sure things happen on time and on budget. It's not glamorous work, but it's taught me a lot about what makes things tick.

## Lessons from the field

**1. Plans are worthless, but planning is everything.**
No matter how detailed your deployment plan is, something will go wrong on the ground. The value isn't in the plan itself — it's in the thinking you did to create it.

**2. Communication is the real infrastructure.**
You can have the best logistics in the world, but if your team doesn't know what's happening, none of it matters. I've learned to over-communicate, especially across time zones and languages.

**3. Small teams outperform big ones.**
Give me 5 people who care over 20 who don't. Every time.

## What this means for my work

These days, I bring this operational mindset to everything I do. Whether it's managing a POSM deployment or building this very website, the principles are the same: **plan thoroughly, communicate clearly, and iterate constantly.**
