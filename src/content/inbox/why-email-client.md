You might be wondering — why does this site look like an email client?

Good question. Here's the thing: I spend a lot of my day in email. It's where conversations happen, where deals are made, and where work gets done. So when I decided to rebuild my personal site, I wanted it to feel familiar and functional.

## The inspiration

I've been a **Mozilla Thunderbird** user for years. There's something about its three-panel layout — sidebar, message list, reading pane — that just makes sense for browsing content. It's information-dense without feeling overwhelming.

## The tech stack

This site is built with [Astro](https://astro.build), a modern static site generator that's perfect for content-focused websites. Here's what I used:

- **Astro** — Static site generation with content collections
- **Vanilla CSS** — No framework, just custom properties and thoughtful design tokens
- **SVG icons** — Inline SVGs for crisp, lightweight icons
- **Google Fonts (Inter)** — Clean, modern typography

The entire site is static HTML — no JavaScript frameworks, no client-side rendering. It's fast, accessible, and works everywhere.

## The design philosophy

The key was getting the details right:
- Dark sidebar with purple accents (just like Thunderbird)
- Light reading pane for comfortable reading
- Message list with sender avatars, dates, and previews
- Action buttons that look interactive (even if decorative)

It's form meeting function — a portfolio that feels like a tool you already know how to use.
